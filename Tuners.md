# Tuners

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; Walked across the way to the bodega. For some Arrogant
Bastard beer, they walked, and walked, and walked. For all of 5 minutes. It was down
the corner and on another one. They walked, down their block and leapfrogged through
an intersection to the uh, the opposing corner. "Walkin' the Double Hypotenuse" they
called it. They didn't know much math, but they knew enough. To make such jokes, they
did. A cackle, a jingle. They sang. The Bottom Dog pullt oot his Harmon & Kardon
speaker and began, for the 2xHypoCross, to play some Mingus or Scotty Lo. Double
Hypo? Some double bass _required_ reading. They called it---the listening of double
bass during a Walkin' Double Hypotenuse---the Quadrangle Jangle Wrangle. I mention
I'm a tuner, too? You ain't gettin' none of our secrets, boy. Best be takin' these
nuggets of in-grouperedness with a grain of glass that's got a smaller grain uh salt
in it. Anyhow, our group delicacy is grouper. And we also enjoy calling 2xBass on the
2xHypoCross a little bit of _required_ reading and so therefore, we call it reading
the paper. Like all our pappies did when we were young and Quadrangle Jangle
Wrangling for the first or second time or. Get the idea or scram!

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; One of them, the head tuner, offered a drink of
choice---for as you all _must_ know, tuners drank in the same key---and they usually
always assented to this choice. Because it was always the same lately: Arrogant
Bastard. But don't get it all confused or spangled, they drank of different
capaciousnesses. A small tuner, why, he'd get a large bottle and the largest tuner
the smallest thimble of Arrogance. They'd recently started up and booted up and
turned over a new scheme. Namely, to grow chest hair for the less fortunate. Arrogant
Bastard was, to their mind, the best manner of keeping turnover high. Chest hair
turnover, if that wasn't clear. The group turnover practically nil. No applicants
being watched for, either, so don't get any ideas. Of the subtractive or of the
additive varieties. This posse knows math jokes, remember? But so anyhow, yes, the
Arrogant Bastard and the chest hair scheme. They were tuners and they crossreferenced
their calipers amongst themselves, from shave to shave. A few kept beards, a few
didn't. The debate was wholesome and lively on no- vs yes-beard. A couple-three tunes
(not to be confused with "songs") adamantly thought that eyeglasses were crucial to
safe shaving (of chest or other) and so either discarded their contact lenses (none
did because they were tuners and didn't like shit in their eyes in the first place)
or kept wearing their glasses (no change from the others' perspective) or, in one
case, "store" (past tense of "stare" (don't ask)) into, yes, the one and only sun for
a chance at glasses. To concreify and solididate (again, terms were made but not by
me) his committment to Sparkle Motion (no comment).
