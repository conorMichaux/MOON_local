# OPC-14597 Signet

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; The anteroom reeked of cleaning products. Doctor Otsuka found the smell
overbearing but comforting. It reminded him of his childhood, spending time in doctors' offices for a
litany of health problems, mostly chronic sinus infections. His pediatrician, Dr. Sawqhardt, was
always a highlight to being sick. Never quite the center or the "reason" for wanting to be there at the
office, but always a plus. His childhood doctor was older, with one pupil in the shape of a tiny Coke bottle,
from a side profile view and as a direct contrast to the usual top-down circular view of everyone else's
Coke-bottle eyes. Many an occasion growing up, Dr. Otsuka would find himself speaking to homeless urchins
with mental illness. He felt drawn to them for some reason and, by analogy, always thought of them as
having a sinus infection of the mind. He therefore wanted to be their Dr. Sawqhardt: the pediatrician
that could comfort, be a plus, a highlight of their day but not the _reason_ for their day. A ray of
clarity for them, someone who knows what the root of their problem is and who can pull it out at that
very same spot.
