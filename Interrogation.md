# Interrogation

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; This one's got drought in him, I can tell by the cut of his jaw!" she shouted
over her shoulder as she entered the room. The man reached into his bag and pulled out a guitar pedal.
"What're you planning to do with that, boy?" It was a flanger. "How'd you get those in here anyway.
Security! Come confiscate his things the way y'all shoulda done when he got here." The man's face
suddenly grew character, as if it had, up to that point, been undefined the way certain math
was. She looked into his eyes and saw the logo of the flanger she had seen on the pedal. Looked away,
slowly, not losing her composure. But not quite keeping it either. Blinked hard a few times, knowing that
he hadn't known her long enough to have a reference for "soft" or "hard" blinking. She leveraged what she
knew and what she knew was that this was a game to him, to them. A drought hadn't happened for some time
and she knew that as soon as he had a window or some line of sight to the sky, he would start dancing
again.

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; That's how they found him. Dancing. Like some sort of savage or Greek. She paused and felt a
memory bubble up to the surface of her mind: the Greeks had something in their mythology about those who danced
for so long that they died and became cicadas. A reward for their celebration of music or something or
other. 

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; The man glanced at her, as if to remind her that he was still there and that she was drifting off into
thought in an interrogation. Was this his magic or something else? If dancers to music became cicadas,
what happens to thinkers to thoughts?  
     

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; Security walked in. Two of them. One woman had tribal tattoos, the other had the kind of septum
piercing that bulls have. "Do you believe in free will?" the man asked her, nodding at her nose. A
cocktail of surprise and mirth spread over her face. "Interrogation's over, lieutenant. This one flanges
emotions. He just did it to her," said tribal tattoos.
    

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; "No the fuck it isn't! Who the shit do you think you are, telling me my interrogation's over just
because some asshat says some off-the-wall shit and makes her blush?" Tribal tattoos shrugged while
septum deviated from her path out of the room in order to pick up the flanger she dropped and asked the
man for the bag, too. He gave her the bag as kindly as he possibly could have, from where the lieutenant
was sitting at least. But she must have missed something, because septum ran out of the rooom screaming and
giggling.   

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; What the lieutenant didn't and couldn't see was that the man made eye contact with septum and showed
the exact mixture of surprise and mirth he had just seen on *her* face.
